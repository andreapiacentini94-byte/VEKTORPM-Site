Services›Temporary Project Manager

Service 01

Temporary Project Manager

When a project needs hands-on leadership, I step in and take ownership of delivery. I work alongside your team, keep things moving, and make sure the project is handed over cleanly when it's done.

When this service fits

This service fits organizations without a dedicated Project Manager facing a project larger or more complex than usual, where structure, progress monitoring, and control over timelines and costs become essential.

As the project's PM, I plan and schedule the activities, define the deliverables across every phase, follow up on progress, and build the reporting that gives governance the right information at the right time. I keep timelines and costs under control, flag risks before they become problems, and act as the single point of accountability with suppliers and internal teams from start to a clean handover. My involvement lasts only as long as the project, giving you the leadership and oversight to deliver successfully without creating a permanent role.

Services›PM Support

Service 02

PM Support

Every organization works differently. I work alongside your team, bringing a proven project structure and adapting it to your environment. The goal is not just to deliver the project, but to leave behind a way of working your team can continue to use.

When this service fits

This service is designed for teams that are already running projects and are looking to introduce more structure and visibility as their work becomes more complex. The work gets done, but planning and reporting tend to happen without a clear method behind them.

I join the project and work with your team. We build the schedule and plan the activities together, get the deliverables clear for each phase, and set up the follow-up and reporting that governance needs to stay informed. I keep an eye on timelines and costs as the project moves, raise risks early, and help manage the relationship with suppliers and the people involved. Along the way I hand the method over to your team, so they can keep running it on their own once I'm gone.

Services›PM Function Implementation

Service 03

PM Function Implementation

If projects are managed differently every time, consistency becomes difficult to achieve. I help establish processes, tools, and ways of working that bring consistency and clarity.

When this service fits

This is for companies that want to build a real way of managing projects, not for one project but for all of them. Usually it's a business that has grown fast and now runs every project its own way, with no shared method to fall back on.

I look at how you run projects today and help create a more consistent and scalable way of working. I define the steps a project goes through from start to close, set up the templates and tools your people will actually use, and decide how projects get approved, tracked, and reported. Then I work with your teams to get them using it for real, not just on paper. Unlike the other services, here I'm not running a project with you on, making sure it holds once I step back.

Services›Process Mapping

Service 04

Process Mapping

Before improving a process, you need to understand how it actually works. I map the current state, identify bottlenecks, and highlight where change will have the biggest impact.

When this service fits

This is useful when work feels slower or messier than it should, but no one can quite point to where the problem is. It happens a lot in companies that are growing quickly, where the way things get done never really kept up with the business.

I sit down with the people who actually do the work and follow a process step by step, the way it really happens and not the way it's supposed to on paper. I map where workflows pass from one person to the next, where they get stuck, and where the same work gets done twice. What you get back is a clear picture of the current state, with the bottlenecks marked and a short list of where to start fixing things. From there, deciding what to improve becomes a lot easier.

Services›Project Intelligence Reporting

Service 05

Project Intelligence Reporting

I turn project data into concise reports that give leaders a clear view of project performance. Progress, costs, and key actions are presented in a format that supports faster, more informed decision-making, with risks tracked in a separate log alongside.

When this service fits

A lot of companies sit on plenty of project data but still can't get a straight answer on how things are actually going. The numbers are there, just scattered across files and tools, and pulling them together every time takes too long.

I take the data you already have and turn it into a report you can rely on. As a first step, we clarify together which business aspects and performance indicators matter most for your decisions, so we have a clear picture of what the end result needs to show. From there, I build a recurring business intelligence report around them: project progress by phase, time and cost tracking, resource utilization, KPIs, risk matrix and whatever else you need. Everything you need to know about the project, always up to date, in a format your leadership can read for fast and confident decision making.

About me

Most organizations have the team, the skills, and the resources to deliver. What’s often missing is someone who owns the project end to end, keeping everyone aligned, knowing exactly where activities and resources stands.

That’s the mission of VEKTOR PM: bring clarity to how projects are run, with the structure, governance, and visibility to keep them under control, so projects move forward without data getting lost across tools or anyone having to chase down what’s actually going on.

Hi, I’m Andrea. I'am an Industrial Engineer and over the years I’ve managed as a Project Manager more than 15 complex international projects across Europe, Asia, North America and Mexico, with budgets ranging from €10 to €70 million and timelines running over several years. My background spans heavy steel plant construction, industrial machinery, and scientific equipment, mostly on brownfield sites where schedules are tight and every solution has to work around keeping the client’s operations running. That’s the kind of setting that has shaped how I work: when a project starts slipping, I get into the detail, rethink how the pieces fit together, and step in directly where it matters, whether that means reorganizing the work across teams or going straight to the source to keep things on track. That hands-on experience is what shaped the way VEKTOR PM works today: bringing structure, control, and visibility into what matters most, so teams can make better decisions and deliver more effectively.

Selected projects

A selection of projects I've led across heavy industry and scientific equipment, from large-scale steel installations to setting up project management practices from the ground up.

PROJECT 01 - NDA
Steel manufacturingCanada

Electric Arc Furnace

I managed the engineering, construction, and commissioning of a meltshop plant consisting in two large EAF units for one of Canada's major steel producers. Beyond the technical scope, the challenge was managing the integration into a plant that never stopped running — sequencing the work carefully and keeping a constant line of communication with the operations team to avoid interference with ongoing production.

PROJECT 02 - NDA
Special steelMexico

Meltshop and CCM

I managed the full delivery cycle of a steelmaking plant and Continuous Casting Machine (CCM) for a Mexican steel producer, from design and manufacturing oversight through to international logistics, construction, and integration into the existing melt shop. The scale of the project, combined with a geographically dispersed supplier network and the constraints of a live production environment, made coordination across engineering, procurement, logistics and site activities a key part of the role.

PROJECT 03 - DALBEN SpA
Scientific equipmentInternational

Astronomical Telescope Production Program

I was brought in to set up the project management structure for a program involving the design and production of a series of astronomical telescopes. The work focused on establishing tracking, reporting, and follow-up practices rather than managing the technical content directly. The main challenge was introducing enough structure to give leadership reliable visibility, without disrupting the way the technical team already worked.

PROJECT 04 - NDA
Stainless steelItaly

AOD Converter System

I followed the development and production of an AOD converter for an Italian producer of stainless and special steels, through to integration with the existing plant. The scope covered coordination between the engineering team, the manufacturer, and the client's operations — managing the handover in a way that minimized disruption to an already running facility.
